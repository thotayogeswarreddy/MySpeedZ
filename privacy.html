Privacy Policy

Last Updated: August 21, 2025

Quick Summary

What we collect: Account info, location during trips, motion activity (to detect driving), driving behavior metrics, profile photos (if you choose to set one), crash reports and diagnostics, and anonymized analytics.

Why we collect: To log trips, provide speed/safety insights, improve features, ensure app stability, and support your account.

Third-party data sharing: Location coordinates are sent to TomTom for speed limit queries. Crash reports are sent to Firebase for app improvement. All data sharing is done securely and according to our partners' privacy policies.

Your control: You can change app toggles at any time, update iOS Location and Motion permissions, request data deletion, export your data, and opt out of analytics via device settings.

1. Introduction

Welcome to Drivile! This Privacy Policy explains how this app ("we," "us," or "our"), as the Data Controller, collects, uses, and shares information about you when you use our mobile application, Drivile (the "Service").

By using the Service, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

2. Information We Collect

We collect several different types of information for various purposes to provide and improve our Service to you. Under regulations like the CCPA, this data falls into specific categories.

a. Information You Provide Directly:

  • Identifiers: Such as your name and email address.
  • Personal Information categories listed in the California Customer Records statute: Such as your name, address, telephone number, driver's license number, and insurance policy number, should you choose to provide them.
  • Account Credentials: Your password for the Service is managed by Firebase Authentication. We do not have direct access to your plain-text password.

b. Information Collected Automatically When You Use the Service:

  • Geolocation Data: Precise GPS location, speed, and heading during active trips.
  • Sensory Data: With your permission, we access data from your device's motion and fitness sensors to automatically detect driving activity.
  • Commercial Information: Records of services purchased, such as your Drivile Pro subscription history, managed via Apple's In-App Purchase system.
  • Inferences: We generate inferences from the data above, such as your calculated driving score and driving style (e.g., "smooth," "aggressive").
  • Photo Library Access: If you choose to set a profile photo, we access your photo library with your explicit permission. Photos are uploaded to Firebase Storage and associated with your account. We do not access your photo library except when you explicitly choose to change your profile photo.
  • Crash Reports and Diagnostics: To help us improve app stability and performance, we collect crash reports and diagnostic information through Firebase Analytics. This includes app crash logs, error messages, device information (model, OS version), app version, and performance metrics (app launch time, memory usage). This data is anonymized and does not include personal information or location data.

3. How and Why We Use Your Information (Lawful Basis)

Under GDPR, we must have a "lawful basis" for processing your data. We use your information for the following purposes based on the corresponding legal grounds:

To Provide Our Service (Based on Contractual Necessity):

We process your data to fulfill our contract with you, which is our Terms of Use. This includes:

  • Operating and maintaining the Service.
  • Logging your trips, calculating your driving score, and identifying driving events.
  • Displaying your trip history and statistics to you.
  • Enabling social features with friends you have approved.

For Business Improvement (Based on our Legitimate Interests):

We have a legitimate interest in improving our Service and keeping it secure. This includes:

  • Analyzing usage to understand how our app is used and how to improve it.
  • Ensuring the security of our app and backend services.

To Comply with Legal Obligations:

We may process your data where it is necessary for compliance with a legal obligation to which we are subject.

With Your Explicit Consent (Based on Consent):

We will ask for your consent before:

  • Sending you push notifications.
  • Accessing your device's location and motion activity data (the OS will prompt you for this).

Automated Decision-Making:

We use automated algorithms to calculate your driving score based on your driving behavior (speed violations, harsh braking, acceleration, etc.). This scoring is used solely to provide you with driving insights and help you improve your driving habits. This automated processing:

  • Is not used for any automated decision-making that has legal or similarly significant effects
  • Does not result in automated profiling that produces legal effects
  • Is based on your explicit consent when you use the Service
  • Can be reviewed and corrected by you at any time

You have the right to request human review of any automated decisions by contacting us at support@drivile.com.

App Store Privacy Labels (Data Linked to You): Identifiers (email/profile), Location (trip use), Usage Data, and Diagnostics. Data Not Linked to You: Aggregated analytics for app improvement.

4. How We Share Your Information

  • With Your Friends: Your public profile information and trip data are shared with users you have explicitly approved as friends. You can manage these permissions in the app's settings.
  • With Service Providers: We use third-party companies to help us operate our Service. These providers are contractually obligated to protect your data. They include:
    • Google (Firebase): For user authentication, database hosting, push notifications, analytics, and crash reporting. Firebase's privacy policy can be found at https://firebase.google.com/support/privacy.
    • Google Maps Platform: For map display and route data. Google Maps privacy policy can be found at https://policies.google.com/privacy.
    • TomTom: For speed limit monitoring, we send your current location coordinates (latitude, longitude, and heading/direction) to TomTom's API servers to retrieve speed limit information for your current road segment. This data is transmitted securely over HTTPS connections. TomTom processes this location data according to their privacy policy, which can be found at https://www.tomtom.com/privacy/. We do not share your personal identity, trip history, or any other personal information with TomTom - only anonymous location coordinates during active API calls when you are using the speed limit monitoring feature.
    • Apple: For processing In-App Purchases for subscriptions. Apple's privacy policy can be found at https://www.apple.com/privacy/.

Payments: All subscription billing is processed by Apple via In‑App Purchase. We do not store or process your credit card details on our servers.

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal data against unauthorized access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security.

6. Location Data and Driving Information

  • Trip Recording: The Service records your location, speed, and driving behavior during trips. This data is used to calculate driving scores and provide trip analytics.
  • Background Location: With your permission, the Service may access location data in the background to detect when you start and stop driving.
  • Data Accuracy: Location data accuracy depends on your device's GPS capabilities and environmental factors.
  • Data Sharing: Your trip data is only shared with approved friends and is not sold to third parties.

Third-Party Location Processing: When you use the speed limit monitoring feature, your location coordinates (latitude, longitude, and heading) are sent to TomTom's API servers to retrieve speed limit information. This processing happens in real-time during active trips and is necessary to provide you with accurate speed limit information. TomTom processes this location data according to their privacy policy. We do not share your personal identity, trip history, or any other personal information with TomTom - only anonymous location coordinates during active API calls.

When Location Is Accessed

  • Foreground: When the app is open, location is used to show your position, speed, and map.
  • Background (Auto‑Trip Disabled): Off unless a manual trip is in progress.
  • Background (Auto‑Trip Enabled): Motion-gated. Only on while driving; off when stationary.

8. Analytics and Usage Tracking

We use Firebase Analytics (Google Analytics for Firebase) to collect and analyze usage data to improve our Service. This helps us understand how users interact with our app and make data-driven decisions for improvements.

What Analytics Data We Collect:

  • App Usage Events: When you start/complete trips, generate reports, change settings, or use specific features
  • Trip Analytics: Trip duration, distance, driving scores, and violation patterns (anonymized)
  • Feature Usage: Which features you use most, map preferences, PDF generation frequency
  • Performance Data: App crashes, error rates, and performance metrics
  • User Properties: Subscription type (free/pro), trial status, app version

How We Use Analytics Data:

  • Improve app performance and fix bugs
  • Understand user behavior and preferences
  • Optimize features and user experience
  • Make business decisions about app development
  • Monitor app stability and error rates

Analytics Data Privacy:

  • Analytics data is anonymized and does not contain personally identifiable information
  • We do not track your exact location, personal details, or trip specifics in analytics
  • Analytics data is processed by Google Firebase and subject to Google's privacy policy
  • Analytics collection is disabled in debug mode to prevent test data from affecting metrics

Crash Reports and Diagnostics:

  • What We Collect: App crash logs, error messages, device information (model, OS version), app version, and performance metrics (app launch time, memory usage, network performance)
  • Why We Collect: To identify and fix app issues, improve app stability, and optimize performance
  • Privacy: Crash reports are anonymized and do not include personal information, location data, or trip details. Only technical information necessary for debugging is collected.
  • Your Control: You can disable crash reporting through your device's Privacy & Security settings under "Analytics & Improvements"

Your Analytics Choices:

You can control analytics collection through your device settings. To opt out of analytics:

  • Go to your device's Privacy & Security settings
  • Find "Analytics & Improvements" or similar
  • Disable "Share Analytics" or "Share with App Developers"

Note: Disabling analytics may limit our ability to improve the app based on usage patterns.
