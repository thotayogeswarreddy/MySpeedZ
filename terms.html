5A. Location Access and Permissions

Drivile relies on location services and motion activity to provide core functionality such as auto-trip detection, trip logging, speed monitoring, and navigation-related features. We follow platform guidelines and respect your choices. The following explains when and why we access these signals:

Foreground Usage (When App Is Open)

  • When In Use Access: While the app is in the foreground, we access your location to display your current position, render maps, show speed and speed limits, and enable navigation-related capabilities.
  • No Continuous Background Requirement: Foreground usage does not imply background tracking. If you close/minimize the app and have not enabled auto-trip tracking, continuous location updates stop.

Background Usage (When App Is Closed or In Background)

  • Conditional Background Tracking: Background location access occurs only when Auto Trip Tracking is enabled and motion activity indicates that you are driving, or when a manual trip is actively in progress.
  • Motion-Gated: We use motion signals (e.g., automotive vs. stationary) to start/stop background tracking. If you are stationary, background tracking is paused.
  • Trip Boundaries: Background tracking stops automatically when the trip ends (detected via motion/activity signals or when you manually end a trip).

Motion & Fitness Permissions

  • Purpose: Motion activity (e.g., automotive vs. stationary) helps detect when to start/stop auto trips without requiring constant location polling.
  • User Control: You can disable motion access in system settings. If disabled, auto-trip detection may not function as intended.

User Controls and Transparency

  • Toggles in App: You can enable/disable Auto Trip Tracking, Sound Alerts, Friend Alerts, Use Google Maps UI, and other features. Changes take effect in real time.
  • System Settings: You can change Location (Never / Ask Next Time / While Using / Always) and Motion & Fitness permissions at any time in iOS Settings.
  • Granular Logging: The app logs toggle changes and subscription context to improve user support and transparency (no sale of personal data).

Data Handling, Retention, and Deletion

  • Trip Data: Trip start/end, route traces, speeds, and aggregate metrics are stored to power analytics and history views.
